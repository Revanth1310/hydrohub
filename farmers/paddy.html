1. Mechanism of Paddy Cultivation

  • Land Preparation: Plowing and leveling for optimal water distribution.
  • Seedling Preparation and Transplantation: Germination in nurseries and transplantation into fields.
  • Irrigation: Use of flooded fields and techniques like Alternate Wetting and Drying (AWD).
  • Crop Maintenance: Application of fertilizers, pest control, and regular weeding.
  • Harvesting: Manual or mechanical methods followed by threshing and winnowing.

2. Water Usage

Traditional methods use 3,000–5,000 liters of water per kg of rice. Modern techniques like AWD and System of Rice Intensification (SRI) help conserve water.

Water-Saving Methods:
  • AWD: Periodic draining reduces water use by 15–30%.
  • SRI: Keeps soil moist rather than flooded.
  • Direct-Seeded Rice: Avoids the transplantation stage, saving water.

3. Suitable Soils for Paddy

  • Alluvial Soil: Rich in nutrients, found in river basins.
  • Clayey Soil: High water retention capacity.
  • Loamy Soil: Well-drained and fertile.
  • Acidic Soil: Usable after treatment with lime.

Optimal soil pH: 5.5 to 7.

4. Difficulties in Paddy Cultivation

  • Water Scarcity: Remedies include rainwater harvesting and efficient techniques like AWD.
  • Pest and Disease Infestations: Managed with Integrated Pest Management (IPM) and resistant seed varieties.
  • Labor Shortage: Addressed by mechanization and farmer training.
  • Soil Degradation: Prevented through crop rotation and organic farming.
  • Environmental Concerns: Methane emissions mitigated by AWD and water-efficient practices.
