1. Mechanism of Sweet Corn Cultivation

  • Land Preparation:
    • Plowing: Deep plowing to a depth of 20–25 cm to loosen the soil and improve aeration.
    • Leveling: A leveled field ensures uniform water distribution.
    • Fertilizer Application: Incorporate well-rotted organic manure (10–12 tons per hectare) and basal doses of fertilizers (NPK: 60:40:40 kg/ha) during land preparation.
  • Seed Selection and Sowing:
    • Seed Selection: Choose hybrid or high-yielding sweet corn varieties suitable for the region.
    • Planting Time: Kharif season: June–July; Rabi season: October–November.
    • Spacing: Row-to-row: 60–75 cm; Plant-to-plant: 20–25 cm.
    • Sowing Depth: Seeds should be sown 2.5–5 cm deep.
    • Method: Use manual sowing or mechanical planters for precision.
  • Crop Maintenance:
    • Irrigation: Sweet corn is sensitive to water stress. Irrigate at critical stages such as germination, tasseling, and grain filling. Avoid waterlogging.
    • Weeding: Conduct timely weeding (2–3 times) to prevent competition. Use pre-emergence herbicides or manual methods.
    • Fertilization: Side-dress nitrogen-rich fertilizers at the 6–8 leaf stage and during tasseling for better yield.
  • Harvesting:
    • Harvest Timing: Harvest when kernels are at the milk stage (typically 18–24 days after silking).
    • Harvesting Method: Use manual harvesting or mechanical corn pickers.
    • Post-Harvest Handling: Cool harvested cobs immediately to preserve freshness and sugar content.

2. Water Usage

Sweet corn requires consistent moisture throughout its growth stages but is sensitive to waterlogging:

  • Water Requirements: 500–800 mm of water during its growth cycle.
  • Critical Stages for Irrigation: Germination, tasseling, silking, and grain filling.
  • Irrigation Methods:
    • Drip Irrigation: Efficient and saves water while maintaining soil moisture.
    • Furrow Irrigation: Commonly used for larger fields but requires proper drainage to avoid waterlogging.
  • Water-Saving Techniques:
    • Mulching to retain soil moisture.
    • Rainwater harvesting for supplemental irrigation.
